The challenge

Campus parking is scarce and fragmented. Students need spots near campus; hosts and property managers have inventory — but one-off listings don’t scale when you’re managing lots, game-day demand, or multi-spot properties. BuyMySpot had to work as a trustworthy marketplace and evolve into SaaS that operators could run inventory through.

My approach

As product owner I ran the roadmap end to end — prioritizing what got built based on customer needs and usage patterns, then reprioritizing as signal came in. I spearheaded the B2B shift (bulk listing, waitlists, demand-facing tools) and pushed the conversion levers that made leases happen: price negotiation and real-time communication. Those weren’t “nice-to-haves” — they are where leases closed 3× faster (first message → signed) and conversion rose 150%.

I directed the technical roadmap in partnership with outside developers — translating customer needs into specs, making the tradeoff calls, and keeping the platform’s services (spots, leases, payments, chat, notifications) healthy as usage grew. And I drove the sales side myself: persistently building the property-manager relationships that turned a student marketplace into a B2B product.

What shipped

  • Discovery & marketing — Next.js site for find/list flows, campus markets (Ann Arbor, East Lansing, Madison, State College), events, and blog CMS
  • Seller inventory — custom date ranges (daily / weekly / monthly / full lease), multi-spot properties, amenities (EV, shelter, passes), vehicle size limits, featured event lots
  • Demand tools — waitlists when inventory matches buyer need; lead capture via EmailOctopus
  • Conversion stack — in-app messaging and price negotiation; lease checkout/finalize; approve/reject on rental requests
  • Money movement — Stripe Connect seller accounts and payouts
  • Seller dashboard — sales, income, upcoming payouts
  • Platform — GraphQL API gateway over Users, Spots, Leases, Payments, Price Calculation, Chat, and Notifications services

How the system works

Dual frontends: a Next.js marketing and discovery layer at buymyspot.com, and a React app at app.buymyspot.com served via S3 + CloudFront. Backend services run as AWS Lambda (SAM) with MongoDB Atlas per domain, aggregated through GraphQL. Auth sits on Cognito. Maps use Mapbox. Payments run on Stripe Connect so hosts get paid without BuyMySpot becoming their bank.

Operator anecdote we kept because revenue concentration matters: one business generated nearly half of their game-day revenue from parking sales through the platform.

How the product works

The product has two jobs: help students find a usable spot fast, and give hosts and property managers enough inventory tooling that supply can keep up with campus and game-day demand.

Surface 01

Discover inventory on a map + list

Goal: reduce time-to-find for students

Find-a-spot put Mapbox pins and a scrollable listing pane on the same screen. Students could scan by neighborhood, price, and amenities without bouncing between a map and a separate results page — the path from “I need parking near campus” to a concrete listing.

Surface 02

Map ↔ list stay in sync

Goal: show spatial search ↔ list sync

Selecting a pin highlighted the matching card; selecting a card centered the pin. That coupling is what made spatial search feel trustworthy — students weren’t guessing which list row belonged to which spot on the block.

Surface 03

Seller tools for bulk inventory

Goal: give property managers tools that unlock bulk inventory / revenue

The B2B surface was where property managers stopped listing one spot at a time. Bulk listing, waitlists, multi-spot properties, and date-range inventory let operators push lots live and capture demand when supply was short — the throughput path behind the ~2.5× listing estimate.

Surface 04

Game-day demand concentration

Goal: event revenue concentration for hosts

Campus seasons aren’t flat. Football Saturdays and event weekends compressed demand into short windows. Featured event lots and flexible date ranges let hosts monetize those spikes — including operators who concentrated a large share of game-day parking revenue through the platform.

Reflection

Marketplaces don’t stall on “more listings” — they stall on trust, speed-to-agreement, and payouts. Price negotiation and chat cut time from first message to signed lease by 3× and lifted conversion 150%, on a platform that generated >$100K for property managers and 1,000+ students looking for parking.
